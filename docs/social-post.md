# Nearfolk — social post draft

I'm building a game about the architecture of kindness.

Nearfolk is a cozy 90-second web game where you design a small cottage cluster around a shared commons, then watch your neighborhood come to life. It's my entry for the Cursor Vibe Jam 2026, a game jam with $35K in prizes where 90% of code has to be written by AI.

The idea came from my ACRE capstone. My partner Chandra Ellis and I spent six months designing Avenues West Cottages, a 15-home pocket neighborhood proposal for a site on W. State Street in Milwaukee. Fifteen small homes around a shared commons, porches facing inward, paths connecting everything. The whole design was built on one idea from Ross Chapin's work: that good neighborhoods happen when people can see each other. Not density. Not square footage. Sightlines. Layered privacy. The porch facing the right direction.

When I finished the capstone I kept thinking about that idea. How the direction a porch faces changes whether two neighbors ever meet. How a tree in the wrong spot blocks a connection. How a shared garden between two homes creates an excuse to be outside at the same time. I wanted other people to feel that, not just read about it. So I turned it into a puzzle.

That's what I mean by "architecture of kindness." The design of a space can make people more likely to notice each other, check in on each other, cross paths on the way home. In the game, that's the score. Place two cottages facing each other, the score goes up. Block the sightline with a tree, it goes down. Leave a resident with no connections, you get a penalty and a sad little cloud over their head.

The part I keep thinking about: a Day Director inspired by RimWorld's storyteller AI, but flipped. RimWorld's Cassandra picks disasters. Nearfolk's Director picks kindnesses. It looks at who's lonely, who hasn't met yet, who has the strongest connections, and writes a small story. "June noticed Maya hadn't been out all week. She brought coffee." Same engine. Opposite feeling.

Two days in. 800 lines of code. 135KB bundle. 8 piece types on a 10x10 isometric grid with golden-hour lighting and tilt-shift blur. The scoring engine already works. That one interaction, two porches facing each other across open space, is the whole thesis. It's the thing I learned at ACRE, turned into something you can feel with a mouse click.

Shipping May 1.
