# Nearfolk — social post draft

I'm building a game about the architecture of kindness.

Nearfolk is a cozy 90-second web game where you design a small cottage cluster around a shared commons, then watch your neighborhood come to life. It's my entry for the Cursor Vibe Jam 2026, a game jam with $35K in prizes where 90% of code has to be written by AI.

This is my first game built with Claude Code. The idea came from my ACRE capstone project, where I designed a pocket neighborhood for a site in Milwaukee. That project got me thinking about Ross Chapin's work on how small communities actually function. His theory is that neighborhoods work because of layered privacy and sightlines, not density. I kept turning that idea over in my head and eventually thought: what if you could feel that as a puzzle? What if you could place cottages, rotate porches, arrange paths, and watch the social connections appear?

So the game doesn't score you on money or efficiency. It scores you on connection. Every porch faces a direction. Every sightline between two porches is a potential friendship. Every tree can save a view or block one. You're arranging space so that good things want to happen in it. That's what I mean by "architecture of kindness." The design of a space can make people more likely to notice each other, check in on each other, cross paths on the way home. That's the score. That's the whole game.

The part I keep thinking about: a Day Director inspired by RimWorld's storyteller AI, but flipped. RimWorld's Cassandra picks disasters. Nearfolk's Director picks kindnesses. It looks at who's lonely, who hasn't met yet, who has the strongest connections, and writes a small story. "June noticed Maya hadn't been out all week. She brought coffee." Same engine. Opposite feeling.

Two days in. 800 lines of code. 135KB bundle. 8 piece types on a 10x10 isometric grid with golden-hour lighting and tilt-shift blur. The scoring engine already works. Place two cottages facing each other, watch the score go up. Drop a tree between them, watch it go back down. That one interaction is the whole thesis.

Shipping May 1.
